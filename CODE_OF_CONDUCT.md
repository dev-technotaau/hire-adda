# Code of Conduct

This document defines the professional conduct expected of everyone with
access to the Hire Adda codebase, infrastructure, and internal communication
channels — employees, contractors, and authorized collaborators.

It complements (and is subordinate to) the company's HR policies and any
employment, contractor, or NDA agreements you have signed with Hire Adda.

---

## Scope

This Code applies whenever you are acting in connection with Hire Adda:

- Contributing to the codebase or infrastructure (commits, PRs, code
  review, deployments, on-call work).
- Internal communication — Slack, email, video calls, in-person meetings,
  ticket systems, design tools, dashboards.
- Representing the company externally — conferences, customer support,
  social media when identified as a Hire Adda team member.

It applies regardless of role, seniority, or location.

---

## Expected Behavior

- **Treat colleagues with respect.** Communicate clearly and professionally,
  including in code review and incident response. Disagreement is fine;
  contempt and personal attacks are not.
- **Assume good intent, then verify.** When something looks wrong in
  someone else's work, ask before escalating.
- **Give feedback on the work, not the person.** "This query will
  N+1" — yes. "You always write bad SQL" — no.
- **Own your mistakes.** Production incidents and bad merges happen. Be
  honest about what went wrong, fix it, and write the postmortem. Blame
  the system, not the person.
- **Respect time and focus.** Default to async (PR comments, issues, Slack
  threads). Reserve real-time interrupts for incidents.
- **Protect customer data and credentials** as if they were your own. See
  the Confidentiality section in [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following will result in disciplinary action up to and including loss
of repository access and termination of engagement:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination based on race, gender, gender
  identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion,
  national origin, caste, or any other protected characteristic.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances in any company
  channel.
- Threats of violence or doxxing (publishing private contact, location, or
  identity information).
- Sustained disruption of meetings, code reviews, or incident response.
- Knowingly committing malicious code, backdoors, or undisclosed data
  exfiltration.
- Sharing customer data, source code, secrets, or internal documents
  outside the authorized team — including pasting into third-party AI
  tools, public chats, or personal repositories.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code, report it
through any of these channels — pick whichever you are most comfortable
with:

1. **Tech Lead** — direct message on Slack or email.
2. **HR / People Ops** — `hr@hireadda.in`.
3. **Founder / leadership** — `legal@hireadda.in` for serious issues
   involving leadership, financial misconduct, or legal exposure.

Reports are treated confidentially. Reasonable steps will be taken to
protect the reporter's identity from anyone other than those who need to
know to investigate and respond.

For security incidents specifically (vulnerabilities, suspected breaches,
leaked credentials), use `security@hireadda.in` instead — those follow a
separate incident-response track.

---

## Enforcement

The tech lead and HR are jointly responsible for receiving reports,
investigating, and deciding on outcomes. Possible outcomes include:

- Informal resolution and coaching.
- Written warning.
- Removal from a specific channel, project, or on-call rotation.
- Suspension or revocation of repository and infrastructure access.
- Termination of employment or contract.
- Referral to law enforcement where conduct is unlawful.

Decisions take into account severity, prior history, intent, and impact.
